The Western Bull Moose, scientifically identified as Alces alces andersoni, is a notable and powerful mammal found in the western regions of North America. Known for its imposing antlers, which can reach widths of up to six feet, the bull moose stands as a symbol of strength in the wilderness. Solitary and herbivorous, these creatures boast impressive sizes, with males often exceeding 1,000 pounds. Their palmate antlers, a distinctive feature, are used for dominance displays during the mating season. Inhabiting diverse environments like coniferous forests, wetlands, and mountains, Western Bull Moose are adept swimmers and have a varied diet consisting of aquatic vegetation, woody plants, and grasses. Despite their vital role in the North American ecosystem, these moose face challenges including habitat loss and climate change.

A black panther is the melanistic colour variant of the leopard (Panthera pardus) and the jaguar (Panthera onca). Black panthers of both species have excess black pigments, but their typical rosettes are also present. They have been documented mostly in tropical forests, with black leopards in Africa and Asia, and black jaguars in South America.

The giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca), sometimes called a panda bear or simply panda, is a species of bear endemic to China.[4] It is characterised by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body. The name "giant panda" is sometimes used to distinguish it from the red panda, a neighboring musteloid.
